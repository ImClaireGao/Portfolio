FNHA WEBSITE

A digital platform to promote culturally safe primary health care services for First Nation Health Authority（FNHA).

Project Brief

FNHA launched the First Nations-led Primary Care Initiative which aims to develop around 15 Primary Care Centres across B.C. to improve access to health and wellness services that are culturally safe and closer to home for First Nations communities. Two of the centres are in operation and the rest are in the development pipeline. FNHA would like to promote the facilities and services of the centres without having patients physically travel there, which often requires a considerable amount of time and travelling costs.

Problems

Based on the research, the problem statement is concluded as “How do we present and promote the Primary Care Centres so that the First Nations communities feel safe and respected to interact with the service?”

Research

Moodboard

One design I created is what I call the City Guide Map style. The layout features an isometric perspective, with muted colors contrasted by highly saturated hues, creating a striking visual effect.

The other design follows a Minimalist style. It’s organized, structured, and simple, with illustrations that use a monochromatic or limited color palette and clean lines. This style is easy to extend and integrate into the existing brand style guide.

Prototype

The map is a Vancouver-inspired island shape with an isometric layout to create a sense of immersion, with BC signature animals to create a subtle connection, such as salmon, orca and bear.
